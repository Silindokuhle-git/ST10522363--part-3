 WEBSITE CHOICE 

SM Operations 

Organization description 

SM Operation’s mission is to tackle the overlooked factors that protect your business from expanding. Our success is based on our ability to please our customers with great customer service efficiently. We are located at the 

heart of Durban UMhlanga operating on a 24/7 basis. 

Why us? 

SM Operations is a reliable international organization that receives over 

50 000 clients a year. I created this web page to inform businesses as well as assist them with their online visibility, as well as the solution to what may be restricting their businesses from expanding and receiving more customers. 

Most businesses face the issue of web pages which are not user friendly or catch the customer’s eye when accessing the page which result in the customer either leaving the website about the businesses uninformed or frustrated because the website is very difficult to navigate and that is where SM Operations takes over. 

Our user-friendly website has several graphics that users can watch to know more about SM Operations as we do understand that some users 

prefer graphics instead of writing. Our pop up for quotation for our business is of great assist to the customers to immediately query the price of our services without having to consult. 

The live display of all our happy clients as well as revenue made from assisting them allows the user to be more at ease because it shows the legitimateness of our services.

     17-Week Timeline and Milestones 

Phase 1: Weeks 1-3 - Business Foundation and Planning 

 Goal: Establish business ideas, service offering, and website purposes. 

Finalize the business concept for SM Operations, including the mission, vision, and target audience. 

Identify the main services offered, such as improving mobile access, updating content, and simplifying menus. 

Draft the business plan and prepare a simple pitch for potential investors or supporters. 

Register or secure the business email and begin planning the brand identity. 

Outline the full website structure, including Home, About, Contact, Why Us, and Client Feedback pages. 

Milestone: Business concept, website goals, and page structure approved. 

 

Phase 2: Weeks 4-6 - Branding and Content Preparation 

   Goal: Prepare all branding materials and written content for the website. 

Design the logo, slogan, and visual identity using the chosen color palette: blue, grey, and white. 

Create supporting website assets such as business cards, thank-you cards, laptop stickers, and Yocco branding visuals. 

Write the content for each website page:  

Home page introduction 

About page story, mission, vision, and services 

Contact page details 

Why Us page 

Client Feedback page 

Collect or create images, videos, and team photographs for the website. 

Prepare quotations wording, call-to-action text, and contact form messaging. 

Milestone: Branding kit and all website content completed. 

 

Phase 3: Weeks 7-9 - Website Design and Development 

 Goal: Build the first working version of the website. 

Develop the website using HTML and CSS. 

Create the layout and styling for all main pages. 

Ensure the color scheme and branding remain consistent throughout the site. 

Add images, videos, borders, forms, and page sections according to the content plan. 

Build the navigation menu and footer with contact details. 

Make the website responsive so it displays properly on different screen sizes. 

Milestone: First full website prototype completed. 

 

Phase 4: Weeks 10-12 - Functionality and User Experience Improvements 

 Goal: Add interactivity and improve usability. 

Add the contact form and test whether enquiries can be submitted properly. 

Integrate the WhatsApp chatbot for easier communication with clients. 

Embed the Google Map on the Contact page. 

Improve user experience by checking page flow, menu clarity, and readability. 

Optimize layout spacing, alignment, and content presentation using CSS. 

Test the site on mobile, tablet, and desktop devices. 

Milestone: Functional website with working interactive features. 

 

Phase 5: Weeks 13-15 - Testing, SEO, and Launch Preparation 

 Goal: Prepare the website for public launch. 

Test all links, forms, buttons, videos, and navigation elements. 

Fix any design errors, broken sections, or content inconsistencies. 

Apply basic SEO optimization, including meta tags, structured content, and sitemap setup. 

Improve page loading speed through image compression and lazy loading. 

Connect the project to GitHub and prepare it for online publishing. 

Review the website against the business goals and target audience needs. 

Milestone: Website tested, optimized, and ready for publishing. 

 

Phase 6: Weeks 16-17 - Website Launch and Early Growth 

 Goal: Publish the website and begin attracting clients. 

Publish the website on GitHub Pages. 

Perform final checks on live pages after publishing. 

Start promoting the business through WhatsApp, social media, and direct business outreach. 

Offer free or discounted UX audits to attract the first clients. 

Begin collecting feedback, quotations, and client enquiries through the website. 

Monitor early performance using basic analytics and visitor tracking. 

Milestone: Website launched and first client outreach campaign started.

Website Pages and Content 

 

Home page 

The front door of the site, the first thing the potential client sees and explains who the business is and where to go next in under 5 seconds. The colors throughout the webpage were inserted using CSS; they were also used to match those of the logo to create uniform look. 

Content it contains: 

Logo- top center, the brand of the business 

Slogan- “Your escape to an effective webpage” keeps business memorable 

The home page also has an image and a looping video that showcases the aesthetics of tech to show the potential clients that are niche is mostly 

tech related. 

I used CSS (Justify content center) to align the logo as well as the slogan, footer and ‘Welcome to SM Operations” to the center. I also used it to put borders around the image and video. 

 

 

 

About page 

This page build trust. People buy it when they know who you are and why you care. The About page is text heavy but broken up with images. Colors stay the same as the Home page for consistency; it functions as a story and proof page. No hard selling but a sort of “this is me and this is why I am not a scammer” 

Links to other pages so people can leave whenever they please 

Contact; same as Home page 

Copyright 2026 

 The about page also consists of all the details that a potential client will need to get to know us. These details include; “The about” information 

Which gives a brief explanation about the business. The mission and vision further explain the core values of the business website. 

There is also a box with bullet points which enlightens the client on what services we provide as a business. 

The lower part of the page consists of a portion which is labelled “Meet our team” and consists of 3 photographs of the individuals who work in the organization; their positions are labelled under their pictures. I inserted the pictures along with their descriptions into separate boxes. Photos give visual representation which assists in customer loyalty and knowing that the business is run by real people. 

I used CSS to align the vision and mission side by side with borders around them. I did the same thing for the “About us” and “Our Services” as well as the “Meet our team” categories. 

 Contact 

 

Although the footer with all the information our potential clients can get in touch with us, this page provides additional information on how the user can get in touch with SM Operations. I inserted a box where you can type in your name, your email address, as well as a message. Our location and map are also included next to the contact details box. 

I used CSS to align the “Contact us” box and map side by side. 

We also used JavaScript to install a pop-up WhatsApp chatbot for easier communication between SM Operations and the client as WhatsApp is the most popular networking platform.  

         

 

 

        Why us?  

 

 This page essentially describes what makes SM Operations different from other          organizations. I centered the box with this description. I also inserted tech related visuals on either side of the “Why us?” box. The looping video at the bottom also allures the user’s attention. CSS was also used to center the text and the image and arrange in the manner that they've been arranged in; I also used it to put borders around this media. 

 

       Client’s Feedback 

This page is essential for constructive criticism from the clients as well as any additional information that they might need. They can also request quotations from SM Operations based on the services of their choice. I 

inserted this fill in form in a box format.

CSS was used to center the “Client’s feedback” fill out form and interactive boxes where the client can fill out their details. It was also used to place 

the images at the bottom as well as putting borders around them.
